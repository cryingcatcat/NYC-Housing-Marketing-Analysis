5. Key Insights

Market Structure
  • Extreme Heterogeneity: Mean ($1.89M) 2.3Ã— median ($825K) indicates luxury-driven market
  • Type Premium: Townhouses command 2.7Ã— co-op prices despite similar square footage
  • Manhattan Dominance: 52% market share creates centralized, high-liquidity environment
Value Drivers
  • Space Over Rooms: Square footage (0.48) correlates stronger with price than bedrooms (0.23)
  • Diminishing Returns: Bathrooms show moderate correlation (0.39); additional units yield less value
  • Location Primacy: Geographic position often outweighs physical characteristics
Investment Implications
  • Buyers: Prioritize $/sq ft and total space; explore outer boroughs for 30-50% savings
  • Sellers: Emphasize neighborhood prestige and space efficiency over bedroom count
  • Investors: Multi-family homes ($1.56M mean) offer balanced entry with income potential
  • Developers: Maximize square footage over room segmentation; open plans command premiums
Geographic Patterns
  • Concentric Pricing: Premium decreases radiating from central Manhattan
  • Borough Opportunity: Queens, Brooklyn, Bronx offer 22% of listings with superior value
  • Market Velocity: 7% pending/contingent indicates healthy turnover, especially sub-$2M

Conclusion

Analysis of 4,780 NYC properties reveals a complex, segmented market characterized by extreme heterogeneity, geographic concentration, and counterintuitive value drivers. Square footage emerges as the strongest price predictor, while property type and location command premiums independent of physical characteristics.
